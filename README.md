
# About me

Hey! I'm Ricardo — a web developer passionate about tech, creating beautiful user interfaces and writing clean & re-usable code. I have a background in graphic design and at my core, I'm a creative problem solver. In my current role, I work as a full stack developer (Laravel / Vue), with a strong focus on creating intuitive and user-friendly interfaces.

I personally prefer working with Golang and Vue, but I'm also comfortable with PHP, Laravel, and other programming languages and frameworks, like C, C#, React and Angular. I'm always looking to learn new things and expand my knowledge.

## My Story

Let's start from the beginning. I was born and raised in a small village in The Netherlands, where I spent most of my childhood playing outside and exploring nature. But I have also always been the computer nerd in my family, playing around with computers when I was just a kid. This early experience sparked a technology flame in me that has remained burning ever since.

During high school, I developed a profound love for art and design. This led me to study Graphic Design in college, where I was introduced to the world of visual splendor and creative thinking.

Throughout my time in college, I was known for my unique blend of artistic flair and tech knowledge. I had this uncanny knack of looking at things from a design viewpoint and then fusing it with tech options to create something amazing.

This unique blend landed me some freelance web development gigs even before I graduated, which cemented my passion and solidified my pathway towards web development.

### Transition to Web Development

After my graduation, my freelance projects started to grow, and I found myself working more on the tech side of things, designing websites and apps. I started realizing that I loved developing more than designing. Intertwining my design skills with my growing passion for coding, I began entering the realm of web development development.

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript became my sturdy trio, allowing me to create stunning interactive websites. I landed a job at [BYRON][], a digital agency, where I worked as a front-end developer. I was responsible for creating and maintaining websites for clients, ensuring they were visually appealing and user-friendly.

One of the most significant projects I worked on was the website of the [Dutch Cycling Embassy][]. I was responsible for the front-end development of the website, ensuring it was visually appealing and in line with the client's requirements. I also worked on the back-end, creating a custom filter system for the website.

After working as a front-end developer for a while, I decided to take the next step and become a full stack developer. I wanted to expand my knowledge and skills, and I knew that becoming a full stack developer would allow me to do that.

I landed my current job at [OmniStock][], a company that develops software for the Omnichannel Supply Chain. I work as a full stack developer, creating and maintaining the company's software.

### What Drives Me

What truly fuels my work is the endless possibilities that web development provides. I love the constant learning and growth it requires; how a new feature can enhance user engagement, how a new technology can improve a website's performance, and how a new design can make a website more appealing.
